Allot Students to our Eduvate specialists from class 5

Students (from age 11-16) will be divided into groups after conducting aptitude tests which help us know where there kids real interests lie.

Monitor Students and log all interests and activities

After being segregated into various groups our specialists keenly monitor the students, in their alloted time slots.

Make note of progress of each student and update it everyday and send it to parents monthly.

Our specialists log all progress at the end of the week and is stored on our database which sends an automated report to the parents at the end of every month.

Analyze data over a course of time and then help student develop in the career of their choice and also ensure holistic growth of students.

All information gathered from regular tests and observations is studied thoroughly over a total period of 4 years and then a detailed report that will help students choose their desired career is sent to both the student and parent alongwith suitable college recommendations.

What we do

  • Our product consists of an interactive classroom framework with activities to showcase various traits of personality, team building, scientific, artistic etc. for a duration of 60 minutes every week included in the school curriculum. We have a team of 1 child psychologist and 1 expert from the 3 main industrial fields and a monthly report submitted to the school and their parents along with hosting a space for interaction of the parents with the team.
  • The concept aims at providing an interactive environment allowing children to express themselves freely, Quantifying personality traits and combining them with academic performances in the formative years, Encapsulating the same as reports ensuring the children and parents are aware of the overall potential and Providing suitable career paths accordingly. It is a quantified report enlisting the traits and abilities of the child to help determine a viable subset of career paths, not based only on the academic index but also the factor of their other skill sets.

Intellectual capability and Cognitive Ability development

  • Development of the abilities to learn from information and experience – this involves learning from data and information; learning from doing an activity, etc.
  • Development of abstract thinking abilities – understanding and thinking about concepts, ideas, principles, and objects which are non-verbal or not physically present.
  • Development of logical and analytical thinking abilities – analyzing reasons behind events and situations, understanding the causes behind an event or situation, etc.
Activities Involved

Emotional Ability development

  • Development of intra-personal abilities – this involves self-understanding (understanding of one’s strengths and weaknesses, one’s capabilities, etc.) and self-belief.
  • Development of interpersonal abilities – abilities to understand others; what others are communicating; building relationships, etc.
  • Development of self-regulation abilities – abilities to control and regulate one’s emotions and feelings.


Activities Involved

Social skill development

  • Development of social competencies – this involves abilities to interact and communicate with others, working in a team, coordination, and cooperation with others, etc.
Activities Involved

Physical capability development

  • Development of gross motor skills – such as walking, running, climbing, etc. Gross motor skills involve the movement of the whole body.
  • Development of psychomotor abilities – these refer to a very wide range of fine motor movements that occurs in response to conscious learning from five senses – mostly from sight, touch, and hearing. For example – painting, cutting of vegetables, making a clay model, typing on a computer keyboard, medical surgery, driving, writing, etc.
